Old Town

In this tour you will explore and the legends and stories of the old town buildings while walking along the narrow and winding cobble stone streets. You will learn about the history and culture of Latvia from the Middle Ages up to present day and discover how the country has changed through architecture. The tour will include visiting such iconic places as The House of Blackheads, St.Peters curch, the Dome Cathedral, Swedish Gate, Big and Small Guilds, Powder Tower and more. Entrances to museums and lunch and/or dinner with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.

Art Neuveau

In this tour you will take a step back hundred years and experience the Art Neuveau architecture which has made Riga world-renowned. Art Nouveau was popular the late 19th century and the beginning of the 20th century and constitute about one-third of all buildings in central Riga. For this reason the centre of Riga is now designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site. As well as Art Nouveau, we will explore the Old Town. If you wish entrances to museums and lunch and/or dinner with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.

Rīga

During this tour you will not only discover the Old Town and the beautiful Art Nouveau buildings, but also visit Central markets. The main attraction is the 5 pavilions, which are so massive that during the WWI they were used as zeppelin hangars. Four of the five pavilions still serve their original function as meat, fish, dairy and produce markets. Nowadays the stalls extend far beyond the hangars, the market has been improved, expanded and renovated over the years, but the bustling market atmosphere still remains. Entrances to museums and lunch and/or dinner with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.

Open Air Museum

If you want to learn more about Latvian history and culture, have a pleasant walk around luscious green forest, see some craftsmen working and learn about our way of life in countryside, it is the place to go for you. Open Air Museum is situated about half an hour away from city centre next to Lake Jugla. There you will find traditionally furnished original wooden houses with thatch roofs, working tools and old vehicles that have been collected and brought over from all around Latvia. If you wish lunch with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.

Jūrmala

The city of Jūrmala is a town, which consists of a string of small beachside resorts. Only 25km away from Riga, you can enjoy the white sand beach (around 33km long), fresh air of the pine tree forest, mineral springs and the beautiful wooden architecture. Jūrmala’s reputation as a spa destination began in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. Wealthy landowners and Russian army officers began the tradition of relaxing at the seaside. In Soviet times it was popular with the Communist officials because of its beach and sanatoriums – holidays were often given as rewards for top union members. It became one of the most popular holiday destinations in the whole Union. The two main beaches (Majori and Bulduri) now bear blue eco-flags signalling their safety to swim in. Entrances to museums and lunch and/or dinner with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.

Rundāles Palace

Located only 65 km from Riga in the mist of the countryside, Rundāle Palace is one the most loved tourist destinations in Latvia by tourists and locals alike. It was designed as a summer residence of Ernst Johann von Biron, the Duke of Courland by Russian court architect F.Bartalameo Rastrelli. The same architect who also designed the famous Winterpalace in St.Petersburg. Its construction began 1730s and 1760s Rastrelli returned to complete it's interior decoration. The Palace boasts beautiful French garden with pergolas, an amphitheatre and a magnificent rose garden, as well as a café pavilion, where you can enjoy champagne, coffee or tea with a cake or a sandwich. A traditional Latvian lunch can be arranged in the White House - the old servants house.

Gauja National Park

In the tour you will explore the picturesque Cēsis, Sigulda and Turaida with its rolling hills and Gauja river valleys. You will visit the ruins of the Cēsis castle, Sigulda castle and the New Sigulda castle. In Turaida Museum Reserve you will visit the oldest wooden church in Vidzeme and its surrounding Livonian cemetery, Dainu Hill, now a sculpture park celebrating Latvian folksongs and partially rebuilt Turaidas castle. You will also hear the story of the Rose of Turaida and visit her grave and the Gutmanis cave (the largest cave in the Baltics) where part of those events took place. The cave still bears inscriptions from as early as the 17th century. During this tour it is possible to take the cable car over the river valley. This tour includes some easy climbing. If you wish lunch and/or dinner with typical Latvian food and drinks can also be arranged.
