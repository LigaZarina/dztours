Useful information

  • + Arrival

    Riga International airport is located only 10km from central Riga. A taxi ride from the airport and centre depending on traffic takes between 10 to 20 minutes and would coast approximately 10 - 15 EUR. You can also take bus number 22 which will take you to the edge of Old Town. Its last stop is opposite the bus terminal next to Old Town. The fare is 2 EUR and it runs every 10 - 20 minutes (see more on transportation and pricing below).

    If you arrive by ferry the terminal is located within a walking distance form Old Town. For those interested there are regular ferry connections between Stockholm and Riga. They are operated by Tallink.

  • + Transportation

    In Riga. There are a few ways how to get around the city. You can use the bus, trolleybus, tram mini bus, taxis or you can rent a bicycle. Public transport operates between 5:30 and 23:30. Night services are also available, though they are a lot less frequent and only for limited routes. Timetables, fares and purchase information can be found at Riga Satiksme. Even though tickets can be purchased from the driver (2 EUR), it is cheaper to purchase an e-ticket at street kiosks and select shops. There are a variety of e-tickets available (including 1, 5 and 10 ride tickets, as well as 24 hour, 3 and 5 day tickets). An e-ticket can be used on all buses, trolleybuses, trams and certain mini buses.

    Be careful, there are frequent ticket checks. Passengers without a valid ticket are subject to penalty fare of 20 to 50 EUR.

    There are a few bicycle companies. Few of the better known are Bicycle Rental, Sixt and Riga Bike.

    Public transport around the country. You can travel to other parts of Latvia by bus or by train. Riga Central Railway Station is located at Stacijas Square, east of the Old Town border. Here you can find more information on timetables and fare. International train connections are also available between Riga and Moscow, St. Petersburg or Pskov.

    The Riga Bus terminal is located east of the Old Town border and west of the Riga Central Markets. Buses ran daily to all the major cities and towns. Here you can find more information on timetables and fare. International bus connections are also available to and from Riga with many major cities (including Berlin, Warsaw, Prague, Kiev, Vilnius, Tallinn, St. Petersburg, Minsk and many more). Three largest international bus services operators are Eurolines and Ecolines.

    With car. If driving to Latvia with your own car major highways connect Riga with Tallinn, St. Petersburg, Moscow, Kaunas, and Vilnius. The speed limit is 50 km/h in urban areas and 90 km/h on rural roads, unless marked otherwise. On some highways the speed limit is 100 km/h. Headlights must be switched on at all times.

    You can also rent a car from most major international car rental companies (including Europcar, Sixt and Hertz).

  • + Emergencies

    The international calling code for Latvia is +371. To contact the emergency services (police, ambulance and firefighters) call 112. Here is a list of Foreign Embassies and Consulates in Latvia.

  • + Voltage

    The electrical current in Latvia is 220 volts AC, 50Hz. European style two-pin plugs are in use.

  • + Shopping and tax free

    The VAT rate is 21% and it applies to most goods and services. Usually this amount is already included in the price. For drugs, books, medical equipment and baby care products the VAT is 12%. You are eligible for tax free shopping if you have a permanent residence in a non-EU country. The minimal purchase amount is 44 EUR. Here you can see full details for tax free shopping in Latvia.

    The largest and most popular shopping centres are Alfa, Spice, Centrs, Stockmann and Domina. Centrs and Stockmann are more popular with tourists as they are in the city centre and easy to get to. You will need to take public transport or taxi to get to the other ones. You can find most major brands in Latvia. However if you want to visit more interesting shops or are just interested in local brands and artists, here is list of good selection of shops. In Your Pocket and Another Travel Guide are two good websites where to look for more information.

  • + Recommended restaurants and cafes

    As with shopping In Your Pocket and Another Travel Guide are two good websites where to look for more information. However below are a few local favourites.

    On a budget. Eating out on a budget is not as hard as you might think, especially if you like to eat like locals whose main meal of the day is lunch not dinner. There are many places around the city that offer lunch specials, often with either one or two dishes and something to drink. Best part it can cost you only around 5EUR. Index Cafe, Miit Cafe, DAD cafe, Muklajs and Andalūdzijas suns all have great prices for lunch and portion sizes are quite decent too. One place where both locals and tourists love to go is Lido. There are a few of them across the city, but the most popular is this one. There you can choose go to the beer cellar, restaurant or the bistro (which is the most popular one). There is also a large recreation park for kids and adults alike. The prices are fairly democratic, but if you go there with a hungry belly, you might get a larger than expected bill.

    Mid-range prices. Rocket Bean Fuel, La Kanna, Mākonis, Cydonia and a beer cafe Valmiermuiža are reasonably priced and are always full with locals.
